# Citizen Function Roles

Status: Canon integration draft.
Issue: #21.

## Core Rule

Citizens are shaped by Function identity. A citizen role is valid only when its labor expresses a Function, supports continuity processing, teaches pressure, or preserves system meaning.

## Primary Operational Functions

### Brown — Fabrication
Brown citizens construct pressure environments, build operational structures, prepare match chambers, and maintain fabrication complexes.

### Green — Continuity Storage
Green citizens preserve returned records, maintain circulation, store process history, and keep replay, Trial, Verdict, and archive material usable.

### Gold — Observation
Gold citizens observe traces, classify patterns, interpret replay history, and expose pressure structures without directly controlling outcomes.

### Stone — Stabilization
Stone citizens validate continuity, enforce locks, preserve truth states, and prevent unstable systems from exceeding survivable limits.

### Blue — Routing
Blue citizens move participants, traces, records, and synchronization states between continuity systems.

### Purple — Processing
Purple citizens transform completed experiences into reusable materials, Verdict cases, Trial fragments, and archive records.

### Pink — Question Formation
Pink citizens expose contradictions, generate Verdict questions, identify uncertainty, and convert hidden pressure into player-facing inquiry.

### Red — Pressure Testing
Red citizens provide escalation, conflict testing, execution pressure, and controlled challenge structures.

### Sophia — Interpretation
Sophia-aligned citizens translate system pressure into player-facing understanding, guidance, onboarding, and survivability support.

## Citizen Class Alignment

- Fabricators: Brown, Green, Stone.
- Trainers: Red, Blue, Green, Purple, Stone, Pink, Gold.
- Observers: Gold, Pink, Green.
- Archivists: Sophia, Gold, Green, Stone.
- Stabilizers: Stone, Brown, Gold.
- Processors: Purple, Green, Blue, Gold.
- Synchronizers: Blue, Gold, Stone.
- Witnesses: Gold, Sophia, Pink.

## Trainer Rule

A Trainer is not an opponent. A Trainer is a continuity citizen configured to teach through Function pressure.

Trainer behavior should reveal the strength, flaw, cost, and consequence of one or more Functions.

## Prohibited Framing

Do not describe citizens as bots, CPUs, filler enemies, or fake players in canon-facing systems.

Implementation may use AI internally, but the player-facing and canon-facing identity is Citizen, Trainer, Witness, or continuity participant.
